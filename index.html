Psilocybe-Subaeruginosa

About

The most common of 8 varieties of psilocybin containing fungi found in New Zealand. Commonly called subs or gold tops.

Descriptive features

  1. Gold/caramel/light brown cap of around 1.5-5cm diamete
  2. 2mm-4mm thick, 30-125mm long white stalk (MUST be white) often stained blue
  3. The gills are crowded, cream color when young, violet brown in age, with adnate to adnexed attachment.
  4. Spore print is dark violet brown

Psilocybe-subaeruginosa

Growing Conditions

  • Subs like to grow in woody areas, like pine bark or woodchipped places
  • It is common in southern parts of Australia and New Zealand from April to August
